# What is LandChain?

Imagine you buy a house. Normally, the government keeps a record of this in a huge central registry. But what if the paper deed gets lost or damaged? What if someone tampers with the central computer database and changes the name on your property?

**LandChain** is a new way to record who owns what property. Instead of relying on a single central database or filing cabinet, it uses something called **Blockchain**.

## What is a Blockchain?
Think of a blockchain as a digital, indestructible notebook that is perfectly copied and shared across thousands of computers around the world at the exact same time. 

- **Permanent:** Once you write something in this notebook (like "Alice owns the house at 123 Main St"), you cannot erase it or secretly change it. 
- **Public & Transparent:** Everyone has a copy of the notebook, so everyone agrees on who owns what. If someone tries to cheat and change their copy of the notebook, all the other computers notice and reject the lie.

LandChain uses this technology to make real estate ownership 100% secure, transparent, and cheat-proof. With LandChain, you don't just trust a piece of paper—you trust a global, unbreakable mathematical system.
